Gross income is the total money a person earns every month before any deductions, like taxes, are taken out. It represents earnings before subtracting expenses.





Monthly car payments are regular, scheduled installments of money made to finance the purchase of a vehicle.

The appraised value of a home is its estimated worth, decided by a professional appraiser considering factors like the property's condition, location, and local sales. It's important in real estate deals, impacting mortgage terms and sale prices.





The estimated monthly mortgage payment is what a homeowner or buyer expects to pay each month for their home loan. This payment includes both the borrowed amount and the interest, typically over a set loan term.





The down payment is the upfront money that a homebuyer pays, usually a percentage of the total purchase price. It's a part of the home's cost not covered by a mortgage loan.





A credit score is a number that shows how financially responsible someone is based on their history. Lenders use it to judge the risk of giving them credit or loans, with higher scores meaning less risk.
